## Trends — down to two group buys, still climbing

**[Prototypist and the multi-project vendor model](/article/prototypist-vendor-spotlight)**

UK vendor Prototypist has spent 2026 running multiple group buys in parallel rather than the one-project-at-a-time cadence most boutique vendors default to — a dual fulfillment-and-house-kit operating model that's proven harder to pull off than it sounds. The piece traces how that structure holds together, and reads it against the tracker's own record of the cooldown: Prototypist eased from a summer peak of 33 to 15 before ticking back up to 20 this week.

Read it if you've wondered how a small vendor manages concurrent projects without the wheels coming off, or you're weighing a pledge and want to know what "multi-project" actually means for your ship date.

## News — a macro pad with an unusual byline

**[OpenAI's first hardware is a Work Louder macro pad](/article/work-louder-openai-codex-micro)**

OpenAI's first piece of physical hardware isn't a phone or a wearable — it's a Codex-branded macro pad built on Work Louder's Creator Micro platform. The piece covers what that collaboration actually shipped and why a software company's hardware debut landed inside the keyboard hobby rather than somewhere more obvious.

Read it if you want the specifics behind a headline that sounds like a joke until you see the board.

## Ideas and builds — half a pound of brass, one more time

**[The brass weight mod: what added mass actually buys you](/article/brass-weight-mod)**

Bolting a weight plate into the bottom of a case is one of the more expensive mods in the hobby for how little it visibly changes. The piece separates what added mass actually does to sound and typing feel from what's mostly marketing copy.

Read it before spending group-buy money on a mod you can't fully explain to yourself.

## Deep dive — the same switch, minus the foam

**[Why the Gazzew Boba U4T Rings Where the Boba U4 Stays Quiet](/article/gazzew-boba-u4t-deep-dive)**

The U4T shares its D-shaped tactile bump with the original Boba U4, but removing the U4's internal foam dampeners changes the acoustic signature more than any spec sheet would suggest. The piece works through exactly what that foam was doing and why its absence reads as a ring rather than a thock.

Read it if you've typed both switches back to back and wanted the mechanism behind the difference, not just the anecdote.

## W30 tracker snapshot

Hall Effect/Rapid Trigger hit a new high this week, up to 94 from 64 eight weeks ago — the tracker's top score, full stop. Keychron's V6 Ultra HE opens its Kickstarter July 22 with a hybrid Nova Socket PCB that reads both TMR magnetic and mechanical switches on the same board, and PC Gamer and other outlets are now describing HE and rapid trigger as the baseline expectation for 2026 gaming boards rather than a differentiator worth flagging. Keychron itself is up to 90 on the same story, after opening $5 deposits ahead of the Kickstarter date.

Split/Ergo layout climbed to 80, up from 52 eight weeks earlier, as MKBGuide and Engadget both name 2025-2026 as the window split layouts went mainstream — both outlets point to the Keychron Q13 Max and Dygma's lineup as the reference boards driving that read. Cherry MX2A ticked up to -46 this week — still net negative, but its first gain in the eight-week window — as MKBGuide's 2026 Cherry guide notes nearly every new Cherry switch now ships on the MX2A stem even as ThereminGoat's dummy-switch review keeps hobbyist skepticism alive.

The week's keycap side belongs to two closings: GMK CYL TA Neo wrapped its group buy July 17 after opening June 19, climbing to 62 on final-week pledges, while DCS Handarbeit closed July 19 and sits at 45 as chatter shifts to its Q4 2026 delivery window.
