GMK CYL Prussian Alert ran at KBDfans from 2026-05-15 through 2026-06-12. The set had been on our trends radar for weeks before the open: the GeekHack interest-check thread cleared 121k views and 141 replies before a single base kit went up for order, putting it ahead of where Metro System and <PartReference id="gmk-cyl-selene" /> were sitting at the equivalent point in their pre-GB curves.

## What's in the run

Prussian Alert is a <Mono>GMK CYL</Mono> doubleshot ABS set in [Cherry profile](/article/keycap-profiles-compared) — the cylindrical sub-line GMK has been running for the last two years on selected sets. The headline palette is a deep Prussian red against an unbleached cream alpha, with a single ochre modifier accent. It reads closer to the warm-Bauhaus end of the GMK catalog than to the saturated dye-sub work showing up in DCS and KAT this year.

The kit structure follows the pattern GMK CYL has standardized across recent runs: a base kit that covers the major ANSI layouts, a modifier kit for ISO and extended coverage, a novelties kit with the design-specific accent legends, and a deskmat. Add-on kits beyond those four are not confirmed in the IC materials — the full kit list with exact key counts is on the KBDfans product page and should be treated as the authoritative source before ordering.

Pricing for <Mono>GMK CYL</Mono> base kits has run between $110 and $155 USD across the last four cycles, with modifier and novelty add-ons typically landing in the $55–$85 USD range each. Prussian Alert's pricing sits within that band; the KBDfans listing carries the exact figures. That range puts a fully-kitted order (base plus at least one modifier kit) comfortably in the $165–$240 territory that has become the standard ceiling for a <Mono>GMK CYL</Mono> enthusiast configuration. Buyers who want the deskmat and novelties on top should factor another $90–$120 into their budget before committing.

The colorway itself is worth a closer look as a buying signal. GMK has produced a lot of red-accent sets over the years, but Prussian Alert's specific red — a dark, slightly desaturated tone that reads almost burgundy under cool light — is not common in the Cherry-profile catalog. The ochre modifier accent is what prevents it from reading as yet another alarm-red set. That combination is the kind of restrained palette that tends to hold its resale value on aftermarket forums better than the high-saturation alternatives, which matters to buyers who are on the fence about whether they will love it in twelve months.

Designer credits and full colorway specifications are on the KBDfans product page.

## Timing

The buy ran 2026-05-15 through 2026-06-12 at KBDfans as the global host. KBDfans was the primary vendor on the IC since January, which was unusual for a GMK CYL set of this profile — Oblotzky and Cannonkeys have historically shared the slot — and worth noting for anyone who typically buys through a regional proxy in the EU or APAC. No proxy partners were listed at publication.

For buyers outside North America, KBDfans does ship internationally, so the absence of regional proxies did not make the set inaccessible — it did mean the customs and VAT handling fell to the buyer rather than a local vendor. EU buyers in particular needed to calculate their local import fees before ordering, as a fully-kitted GMK CYL order clearing customs in Germany or France can add 20–25% to the landed cost. If regional proxies were announced after publication, the GeekHack GB thread and the KBDfans product page would have carried them first.

On production timeline: GMK CYL sets have been running roughly 9 to 12 months from GB close to delivery across the last three cycles. A close in mid-June 2026 puts estimated delivery in the Q1–Q2 2027 window, which is consistent with what buyers should expect from any manufactured GMK set at the current queue depth. There is no shortcut — the cylindrical tooling requires the same production scheduling as standard Cherry — but the estimate is predictable, which is more than can be said for some of the smaller-batch import sets competing for attention this cycle.

KBDfans's recent <Source href="https://kbdfans.com/products/gmk-cyl-king-of-the-seas">GMK CYL King of the Seas</Source> cadence held: Prussian Alert's order window ran four weeks after the open, in line with that precedent. The closed entry is archived on the [/group-buys/past](/group-buys/past) board.

## Why this is on the radar

The trends signal is concrete. Prussian Alert was tracking at +38 on the [2026-W19 Trends Tracker](/trends/tracker/2026-W19) in the week before the buy opened, direction up, on the strength of IC traffic heading into the GB. That is a score normally held by the breakout switch of the moment, not by an unreleased keycap set.

The palette is the second reason. The red-on-cream colorway is doing what GMK CYL Olivetti did in earlier runs — leaning on a small, specific design vocabulary rather than a kit-everything modifier sprawl. Prussian Alert's IC renders pair the alphas with a tight Cyrillic + Hangul + 40s sub-kit and very little else. Compared to GMK community staples like <PartReference id="bento" /> R2, which earned its reputation on a similarly tight palette story, the editorial discipline shows in how few accent colors GMK sampled before locking the run.

## What we were watching

Three questions rode on the close. First: whether KBDfans posted a single global SKU or split regional fulfillment — a single SKU usually means tighter allocation and an earlier close. Second: the modifier-kit count — CYL sets that ship with three or fewer kits tend to keep their pricing tight, while sprawl correlates with deeper discounts at restock. Third: whether the deskmat and novelty kits ran as add-ons to the base, or were held back for a future add-on round.

On reception: the IC numbers said the demand existed going in. The open question is whether GMK CYL as a profile sustains a second strong year, or whether enthusiast attention rotates back to non-Cherry profiles ([DCS Olivetti's recent close](/article/dcs-olivetti-comeback) already hints in that direction). Follow the [trends tracker](/trends/tracker) for post-close movement data. On the artisan-resin side of KBDfans' catalog during the same open window, [GSK Sweet Nightmare](/article/gsk-sweet-nightmare-group-buy) ran a four-day eight-variant group buy from 2026-05-08 through 2026-05-11 — a different buy shape and production model from GMK CYL, but the same host.
