GMK CYL Prussian Alert is set to open at KBDfans on 2026-05-15. The set has been on our trends radar for weeks: the GeekHack interest-check thread cleared 121k views and 141 replies before a single base kit went up for order, putting it ahead of where Metro System and Selene were sitting at the equivalent point in their pre-GB curves.

<Callout type="note" title="What's confirmed, what isn't">
  KBDfans has named the set and the 2026-05-15 GB-open date. Final base-kit pricing, regional proxies, and lead time were not published as of this writing. Treat any number outside the GB date itself as unconfirmed until the product page goes live.
</Callout>

## What's in the run

Prussian Alert is a <Mono>GMK CYL</Mono> doubleshot ABS set in Cherry profile — the cylindrical sub-line GMK has been running for the last two years on selected sets. The headline palette is a deep Prussian red against an unbleached cream alpha, with a single ochre modifier accent. It reads closer to the warm-Bauhaus end of the GMK catalog than to the saturated dye-sub work showing up in DCS and KAT this year.

Designer credits and the kit list (base, modifier kits, novelties, deskmat) sit on the GeekHack IC and will mirror onto the KBDfans product page when the buy opens. The set is GMK-manufactured, so the usual GMK lead times apply — long, but predictable.

## Timing

The buy opens 2026-05-15 at KBDfans as the global host. KBDfans has been the primary vendor on the IC since January, which is unusual for a GMK CYL set of this profile — Oblotzky and Cannonkeys have historically shared the slot — and worth noting if you typically buy through a regional proxy in the EU or APAC. As of publication, no proxy partners are listed.

If KBDfans's recent <Source href="https://kbdfans.com/products/gmk-cyl-king-of-the-seas">GMK CYL King of the Seas</Source> cadence holds, expect a 4-to-6-week order window after the open date. The live entry will land on the [/group-buys](/group-buys) board when the product page goes up.

## Why this is on the radar

The trends signal is concrete. Prussian Alert sits on this week's [/trends/tracker](/trends/tracker) board at +38, direction up, on the strength of the IC traffic heading into the GB. That is a position normally held by the breakout switch of the moment, not by an unreleased keycap set.

The palette is the second reason. The red-on-cream colorway is doing what GMK CYL Olivetti did in earlier runs — leaning on a small, specific design vocabulary rather than a kit-everything modifier sprawl. Prussian Alert's IC renders pair the alphas with a tight Cyrillic + Hangul + 40s sub-kit and very little else. Compared to GMK community staples like <PartReference id="bento" /> R2, which earned its reputation on a similarly tight palette story, the editorial discipline shows in how few accent colors GMK sampled before locking the run.

## What we're watching

Three things will tell us where Prussian Alert lands when the dust settles. First: whether KBDfans posts a single global SKU or splits regional fulfillment — a single SKU usually means tighter allocation and an earlier close. Second: the modifier-kit count. CYL sets that ship with three or fewer kits tend to keep their pricing tight; sprawl correlates with deeper discounts at restock. Third: whether the deskmat and novelty kits run as add-ons to the base, or get held back as a future add-on round.

On reception: the IC numbers say the demand exists. The question is whether GMK CYL as a profile sustains a second strong year, or whether enthusiast attention rotates back to non-Cherry profiles (DCS Olivetti's recent close already hints in that direction). We will revisit when the GB closes and the order numbers settle.
